# UIUC Course Vault: Sharing Platform for Study Materials

# **Introduction**

As a student at the UIUC, I have both benefited from additional study materials during exams and found myself unprepared for exams because of a lack of resources from previous students. When preparing for exams in the courses, I noticed that professors typically only provided simple templates and guidelines. However, some students can access past exam papers, detailed study guides, or notes through small circles such as fraternities, student organizations, or alumni connections. These resources often circulate within closed circles. For students with smaller social circles or busy schedules, these more detailed study materials are often difficult to obtain or require a significant time investment. I once had to rely solely on the brief study guide provided by the professor to prepare for a final exam until a friend invited me to join a Discord study group. The group provided many past exam questions and detailed materials, which helped me pass the final exam.

These experiences made me realize that the imbalance and scarcity of high-quality learning resources significantly hinder many UIUC students preparing for their exams. Therefore, I hope to establish an open-source platform for sharing UIUC course resources. I want to develop this platform because I believe that course-related materials at UIUC should be a public resource for all UIUC students, not just a benefit for a select few. I hope this platform will help groups who struggle to access relevant academic resources, including international students from other regions, freshmen without established networks, transfer students unfamiliar with the campus environment, and students who haven’t joined organizations that provide study materials. But more importantly, all students will gain access to more study materials through this shared knowledge platform. An active, stable, and open course resource-sharing platform can help students in future semesters and even assist faculty in collecting and preserving teaching materials. I aim to build an open-source learning community where all UIUC students can contribute to and benefit from it, ensuring that every student has sufficient resources to support their courses.

# **Project Vision and Goals**

The platform is named “UIUC Course Vault,” intended to serve as a repository where UIUC students can store valuable materials. As an open-source platform, it aims to serve as a centralized repository for student-created and curated study materials across all UIUC courses. Its core objective is to organize, preserve, and continuously update high-quality learning resources contributed by individuals, such as annotated lecture notes, compilations of past exam questions, topic summaries, and exam strategy guides. All UIUC students will be able to browse and download these resources freely.

The platform is designed around the core concept of an open-source Git repository. The platform will be maintained by two types of community members: managers and maintainers. The associate deans of all colleges will serve as managers; they have the authority to grant maintainer privileges and make final decisions on user requests. Maintainers are students and faculty members within each department who have excellent academic performance, made significant contributions to the community, and have been recognized by managers. UIUC students can access documents and other learning materials on Vault after verification. Every document in Vault retains a version history. Users can download previous versions of documents. Additionally, all users may upload updated versions of documents or new course materials. When a user submits a request, it is sent to maintainers as a “pull request.” Maintainers review the submission and, after confirming that the submitted resources are essential for the course, merge or add them. Both the original author and subsequent contributors retain attribution rights for their respective versions, and approved maintainers are also credited. This model encourages incremental improvements and maintains a clear audit trail to establish accountability and credit.

We hope that the UIUC Course Vault will transform the current situation where student-created learning resources are overly closed off, shifting from a closed to an open model. By making the contribution process visible and rewarding, the platform encourages students and faculty to become active participants in building a sustainable public repository and to benefit from it. Its long-term vision is to become a student-maintained platform. It will serve students and be trusted by students.

# **Target Audience and Stakeholders**

The primary users of UIUC Course Vault are undergraduate students at UIUC enrolled in courses that include midterm or final exams. This group encompasses nearly all UIUC students. Additionally, it’s particularly suitable for international students, freshmen, transfer students, and students who are less involved in campus social life because the platform makes all materials publicly available.

The platform’s key users are the contributors. They can upload original notes, create study guides, correct errors, and submit content updates. Their willingness to share, embodying the open-source spirit, is central to our platform’s philosophy. Furthermore, we hope this Vault will provide them with recognition from other community members and opportunities to deepen their own understanding. The platform’s design must ensure their experience is seamless, rewarding, and sufficiently engaging.

Another user group is professors and teaching assistants. Although the platform is primarily based on student-generated content, professors’ support is decisive. The faculty of the courses can determine whether certain materials are approved for sharing or provide official example problems, thereby collaborating with students to build the community. Their trust is the cornerstone of the platform’s legitimacy. The platform must clearly block materials that instructors explicitly request not to be shared and distinguish between official and unofficial resources.

# **Core Features**

UIUC Course Vault will effectively categorize and manage version control for user-uploaded content. We will provide a repository-like space for each course. The Vault will organize courses in the order of Year-Semester-Alphabetically Sorted Course Code-Course Number (similar to Course Explorer). Within each course, documents are organized into a file tree based on Exam Type-Content Type. For example, if a user wants to find study materials for the Fall 2025 final exam for “Social Aspects of Information Technology” (IS 202), they would locate the course under 2025-Fall-Information Science-IS 202, then click the “Final Exam” folder to access past exam papers, review outlines, and other content. Each resource is labeled with the number of revisions, version history, contributor and maintainer credits, and a community rating.

When a student uploads a new resource, it will be marked as “unofficial” and entered into the repository as an initial submission, open to everyone. Others can suggest improvements, such as correcting typos, adding solution steps, or reformatting the document. These suggestions are submitted as modification proposals for the current version. The course maintainer reviews these proposals and merges them, and marks the resource as “official after ensuring their accuracy and compliance with university and community guidelines. Past versions of documents are retained as historical versions. The complete revision history is always publicly visible, allowing any user to see exactly what was changed. All changes are reversible and traceable; this transparency accurately identifies contributors and responsible parties during the modification process, fostering trust between users and community staff. The platform also supports branching, allowing other contributors to create independent versions of similar learning materials within the same document folder. These versions are initially labeled “unofficial” but can be recognized by maintainers as independent versions of the same material. Users can select their preferred version. Additionally, we will provide a small discussion platform. Its core function is to aggregate all contributors’ suggestions for platform development, such as reporting, sharing improvement goals, and learning from others’ perspectives. These methods distribute decision-making power across the entire contributor community rather than concentrating it in the hands of a few maintainers.

# **Open Source Philosophy**

UIUC Course Vault will be open-sourced in two ways. First, the platform’s source code, including the front-end interface, back-end database logic, version control engine, and API, will be publicly released on GitHub. This ensures that any derivative projects or branches of the platform itself remain open-source; even if the founding team steps away, faculty and students within the UIUC community can continue to improve the platform. We hope it will allow individuals to contribute to the platform’s development based on their own abilities. However, to safeguard the platform’s functionality and usability, all user-uploaded content will be labeled “unofficial” to alert users to the potential risk of bugs. Once tested and approved by the responsible developers, the label will be removed and the content merged, becoming an official feature. We will also provide open communication channels, such as discussion boards and tracking systems, to share ideas and feedback, supporting a more dynamic and agile development process. Second, the platform’s content will allow students to use, share, and adapt learning materials, provided they properly cite sources, do not use the materials for commercial purposes, and share any derivative works under the same terms. This license preserves the platform’s spirit of open sharing while protecting contributors’ right to attribution, helping to ensure the project’s long-term sustainability. The open-source ethos is a core value in our development and construction of this course resource-sharing platform. We believe this platform belongs to the UIUC community and must not be privatized or subject to paywalls. The community can fork and continue to maintain it. We hope to attract all developers who want to share knowledge and help students perform better academically to join this platform and collectively maintain its open and transparent environment.

# **Contribution Process**

The contribution workflow is designed using the pull request model, which is the most common and stable practice in the open-source software community. Users who wish to improve existing documentation or upload new documents should first fork the repository to their personal workspace. After making modifications, they should include a brief description of the changes and their rationale, then submit a pull request. Course maintainers will review the request. Maintainers may request documentation sources, suggest further modifications, or directly approve and merge the changes. This communication process is public and traceable, creating a record of how the final version of the documentation was formed.

This model also applies to code contributions to the platform itself. Developers fork the codebase, implement new features or fix bugs, and submit pull requests reviewed by developers. The code first undergoes code checks and unit testing via automated testing software for each pull request; if issues arise, the request is rejected immediately with an explanation. After the machine review, the responsible developer tests the relevant feature module for the contributor’s request and approves once stability and usability requirements are met. If the code exhibits poor reusability or compatibility issues, the contributor should be asked to refactor the code to ensure compatibility with existing features. If the contributor agrees, the refactored code is merged into the existing codebase; if they refuse, the request should be rejected.

# **Community Governance and Development**

The governance mechanism of the UIUC Course Vault will not involve excessive layers of hierarchy, but it will be clearly documented to establish accountability. Every user entering the platform repository will see a pinned process document outlining the decision-making process, who has the authority to merge content or code changes, how to become a contributor, and how to obtain maintainer status. The selection of maintainers is based on academic performance, contribution history, and alignment with the community’s values of openness and inclusivity. All relevant criteria are publicly available to all users. Additionally, the platform will publish community guidelines; users need only comply with UIUC regulations and refrain from using platform resources for commercial purposes. Maintainers are responsible for reviewing daily requests and organizing documents, though administrators may override a maintainer’s decision. However, major decisions—such as banning users with multiple violations from the platform—will, whenever possible, involve a democratic vote among all administrators and maintainers. Furthermore, before implementation, feedback and votes must be solicited from all users via the forum; the decision can only be enforced if more than 50% of users (excluding community administrators) approve it.

Community development will combine online and offline strategies and reward users who make significant contributions. Users may edit their display names, but access to the platform requires UIUC identity verification. The platform itself will recognize outstanding contributors through prominent acknowledgments, records of continuous contributions, and other potential material rewards. We can collaborate with on-campus academic support departments, such as Career Development, to raise awareness of the platform. I envision organizing a “Study in Vault” event each semester. In the second week of each semester, we will collaborate with course instructors to distribute flyers, ensuring all students are aware of the platform. We will establish a Discord server for real-time discussion and communication. All channels will adhere to a code of conduct emphasizing respect, inclusion, and openness.

# **Roadmap**

The development roadmap for UIUC Course Vault is divided into three phases. The short-term phase (first 4 weeks) focuses on completing a small demo with core features and collecting foundational course materials as initial content. Early milestones include: a usable course catalog and basic version control for documentation, thereby enabling the platform’s core functionality. Mid-term goals (weeks 5–8) include implementing a pull-request review workflow, establishing a contributor forum, and identifying at least 5 core contributors and 2 maintainers. The final phase (weeks 9–12) requires obtaining university authorization, introducing a dean as an administrator to oversee the certification of learning materials, and verifying functional reliability. During weeks 13–16, a small-scale pilot will be conducted in the more subjective College of Humanities and Social Sciences to test student satisfaction, workflow organization, and platform stability. Following a successful pilot, the platform will be opened to all UIUC users.

# **Risk Management**

We need to guard against certain potential risks during development and subsequent operations.

First is the issue of academic integrity. The platform must have a robust review mechanism to ensure the reliability and originality of learning materials and to identify contributors clearly. The platform must also have a takedown process to ensure that materials that violate academic integrity—such as plagiarized content, errors, or materials not authorized for public release by instructors—can be promptly removed. Additionally, the platform will clearly label materials as official or unofficial to reduce the risk of students being confused about the materials’ authority.

The second risk is technical debt. As new features are added, the code may become difficult to maintain and scale. Regular code reviews should be incorporated into the development process. By enforcing code reviews and performing periodic refactoring, we aim to ensure that technical debt remains visible and manageable.

The third potential risk is the turnover of platform administrators. Since most maintainers and contributors are students, the platform’s long-term sustainability establishes a sustainable maintenance model, ensuring that projects can be fully handed over to subsequent student maintainers after senior students graduate.

From the outset, we will prioritize building a comprehensive repository of developer documentation. This repository should include critical community content such as code documentation, community processes, governance guidelines, and institutional knowledge. We will also collaborate with managers to create a maintainer’s handbook to help new maintainers understand their responsibilities. When new maintainers join, we will assign senior maintainers to mentor them and review their work.

# **Conclusion**

UIUC Course Vault is the open-source knowledge-sharing platform we have launched. We will adhere to the community principles of transparency, progress, and co-management. By applying Git’s version control logic to learning materials, the platform can preserve past learning resources and provide high-quality exam preparation materials for every student. To ensure the successful development and governance of this platform, we need to define the scope of development, establish clear development processes, and create a governance structure capable of long-term iteration. Although some risks cannot be ignored during development and operation, we are confident that we can complete the development and create an excellent learning community. Most importantly, all of us administrators must always keep in mind the platform’s original goal: to provide shared learning resources for all UIUC students. This is the outcome that makes our efforts worthwhile.
