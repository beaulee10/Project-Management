Project Vision:

The project vision is to reduce long wait times and improve quality care and experiences in the hospital's emergency room departments. By using the triage system to estimate the average wait time for each patient based on their vitals and symptoms, this system will provide transparency. The goal is to relieve frustration and provide quality care by providing patients with accurate wait times to see a healthcare professional. 

Abstract
Triage level pain system is designed to relieve patients frustration by providing estimated wait times in the emergency room with a patient dashboard in the waiting room and a healthcare provider dashboard.. This project focuses on 3 project management principles, stakeholders and target users, project scopes, project phases, system designs, project phases, project management strategies, risks and ethical considerations, sustainability. This project is structured for about 9-16 weeks depending on development and feedback processes. 


Introduction
Have you ever been to the emergency room and waited a long time to see a doctor? The emergency room can be one of the most overwhelming experiences while you are in pain. The beginning stages of the emergency room process need to be reevaluated. The first step is walking to the front desk of the emergency room and checking in and verifying your information. After that you're asked to sit down in the waiting room to be called for triage. A healthcare professional will call you into the triage room to check your vitals and ask about your symptoms and pain levels. After your vitals are taken you are then directed back to the waiting room. It could be hours before you are called back by the doctor. The triage-based system is designed to estimate the average wait time based on the symptoms to prevent frustration and frequent questions to the front desk.

Wait time transparency matters in various contexts for both patients and healthcare providers and professionals. The current system that is in place in the hospitals’ emergency rooms is not as efficient as people think. I have a strong interest in the healthcare industry and I am dedicated to making healthcare systems more efficient and better for all. I have experienced the current emergency room system of not knowing when I’ll see a healthcare provider and having to constantly ask the front desk how much longer I have before I am seen.  This has caused me frustration while I am also in pain. This project is something that I am passionate about and want to improve in the near future. 


Purpose
The purpose of this triage system is to reduce the patient's frustration. In the emergency room, there’s a lot of emotions while being in pain. Patients come into the ER for various reasons and all of them have a place on the triage level. The triage system will have 5 main categories: Emergency(Life Threatening) , Very Urgent, Urgent, Less Urgent and Non-Urgent/Emergency. The purpose of this is to categorize the patients based on their symptoms. The triage level is designed to determine the wait time based on your symptoms and vitals.

Scope Management has two main components, in-scope and out-scope of what the triage system is designed for and what it is not designed for. The system focuses only  on predicting wait times based on patients' vitals and symptoms and not predicting medical diagnosis and decision making. Stakeholders + Target Users
The stakeholders and the Target Users for this open source project are patients, healthcare professionals, hospital administrators, and open-source contributors. Each stakeholder plays an important role in this project. Patients are the main contributor in this project by providing their vitals and pain symptoms. Hospital administrators play a vital role in contributing to this project. However, the team of developers and researchers are equally responsible as healthcare professionals. The research team will research and develop the success and failure of the current triage system and recommend recommendations based on their findings. The development team is made up of ux developers, data scientists, and software developers to ensure dashboards are displayed accurately. The target users will be Healthcare professionals and patients in the emergency room department. Healthcare will use the data from the new triage system to display and provide information to a patient based on their vitals and current systems. 

Project Scope:
The In-scope part of this project is wait time predictions using vitals and symptoms and the user interface for displaying estimates. The vitals and symptoms are the most important elements of this project. Vitals are important because it helps determine the evaluation of your medical treatment, gives a baseline of your current health status and helps identify if there are any medical emergencies or signs. Symptoms are important because it can be an early detection of health issues, preventing further complications and how the patient is currently feeling. Both vitals and symptoms play a big role in the triage system; it helps determine if you have an emergency or non-medical emergency right away.  This project will display the amount of time you have left before receiving treatment. 

The out scope of this project will not replace diagnosing patients and replacing medical decision making. The triage system is to improve the quality of care not to determine a diagnosis based on triage symptoms. This project will not determine what medical advice you should take and it will only provide you with your current wait time to see the doctor. A triage system will not replace the medical advice and healthcare professionals can give you because it is outscope of what it is designed to do. It is only to predict how much time you have left before receiving care.

Project Phases:

Phase 1 : Initial + Planning
The data collection process starts. I will collect data from the patients and different healthcare professionals. The type of data will include qualitative and quantitative responses on the current emergency room triage system that is in place. This project will take about 1-3 weeks to collect a wide range of data and feedback.  We want different perspectives from stakeholders. In this process, all of the symptoms will be categorized into the 5 main levels of the triage symptoms for the patients dashboard. 
Phase 2: Analysis + Design
After all the data is collected from everyone, the data analyst will begin to analyze and identify key issues within the current system and provide recommendations to bridge the gap. This will also be the beginning of the design process with ux designers and software engineers. This is the stage where the design of the new triage system and the redesign of the current triage intake system. The front team will design 2 dashboards one for patients and one for healthcare providers.  The current intake system for triage is not ideal and based on feedback from data collection it will be redesigned to ensure better quality and accuracy. After all of the designs and recommendations take place it will move into the review process by both the technical and 
healthcare team. This process will take about 2-3 weeks. 

Phase 3: Development + Implementation:
This phase is where UX/UI designers and software engineers develop the triage system that displays wait times. UX/UI designers will research and develop the interface using the software Figma to create and design the dashboard layout. After the triage system is designed, the software team will take over. The frontend team will implement the web development part of the triage process using html, javascript and css. The backend team is responsible for making sure the system complies with HIPPA regulations. Additionally, the backend team will build and manage data to ensure patients data is accurate and updated at all times. The development of the triage system is 4-6 weeks. The triage system will cover a wide range of symptoms

Phase 4: Testing + Evaluation
This is the final phase of testing the newly developed triage system. The testing will be tested by the collaboration team as well as stated stakeholders. The evaluation will come from the patients and healthcare providers. Feedback will be collected on what needs to be improved and how adjustments can be made. Testing will also be done on various symptoms because you can have multiple symptoms in different levels of the triage system. This phase will be 2-4 weeks.

Sustainability
For the triage system to remain sustainable it needs to be updated regularly, have ongoing contributors contributions, and have structured governance maintenance . The reasons why the triage system needs to be system updated are the following: patient data changes, the hospital workflow shifts or changes, and the wait time accuracy improves. Automation plays a key role in the triage process. In Unit 5, we talked about the pros and cons of Automation and the triage system focuses on streamlining processes, increased predictability and improved quality control(Automation). However one of the cons is that it takes humans out of the loop but for this project, humans are one of the most important parts because of the healthcare providers and patients feedback. Automation will help with the streamlining process making it more efficient ensuring that there is no duplication of things and removing unnecessary steps in the development process. Increased predictability is one of the most important things Automation can do for the triage system. The backend team develops the algorithm that helps predict the wait time estimates. 

Conclusions

This project proposes a triage-based system designed to improve transparency and efficiency in emergency room wait times. By using patient vitals and symptoms, the system provides real-time wait time estimates to help reduce uncertainty and improve patient experience. The system supports healthcare providers by improving queue management and prioritization while maintaining a clear boundary between operational support and medical decision-making. Overall, this project aims to enhance communication, improve workflow efficiency, and create a more transparent and user-centered emergency care experience. However, AI can be a great advantage for this project. It can help streamline human services for other humans with the help of AI. If a structure or design is unclear, AI can be helpful to help navigate challenges. I am excited to work on this project, because it allows me to make a difference in such a small impactful way.
